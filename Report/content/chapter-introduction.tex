\section{Research goals}
The aim of this thesis is to decrease the dielectric loss and noise of amorphous dielectrics for superconducting resonators, by optimizing the deposition process. Apart from measuring the loss and noise in the GHz as well as THz frequency ranges, the films are characterized with ellipsometry, Fourier transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy and Raman spectroscopy. 

A correlation of the loss and noise properties with the material properties, that are determined by the characterization measurements, may increase our understanding of the origin of the dielectric loss and noise. Additionally, it will enable us to accelerate the optimization process, because the characterization can be performed on the dielectric layer immediately after deposition, in contrast to the loss and noise measurements that require the dielectric to be part of a superconducting chip.
