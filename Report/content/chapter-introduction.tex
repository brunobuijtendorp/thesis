\section{Research goal}
The goal of this thesis is the deposition of a dielectric thin film with optimal loss and noise in the GHz and THz frequency ranges. The loss and noise properties are measured using superconducting resonators. The dielectric layers are characterized using ellipsometry, Fourier transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy and Raman spectroscopy. By doing so, a correlation can be made between deposition parameters, material characteristics, and loss and noise properties.
